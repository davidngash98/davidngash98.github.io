Meat Cut Guide

Sirloin

Sirloin is a flavorful and tender cut of beef known for its tenderness and balanced flavor profile. It is typically located in the hip region of the animal and is versatile for various cooking methods.

Cooking Tips

For a perfect sirloin steak, preheat your grill or pan to high heat. Season the steak with salt and pepper and cook for about 3-4 minutes per side for medium-rare. Let it rest for a few minutes before serving.

Recipes

Try our delicious sirloin steak recipes, including classic sirloin with garlic butter, sirloin stir-fry and grilled sirloin with a red wine reduction sauce.

Pairing Suggestions

Pair sirloin with red wines such as Cabernet Sauvignon or Merlot. Sides like roasted potatoes and sautéed vegetables complement the steak's rich flavor. For sauces, consider a classic béarnaise sauce.
